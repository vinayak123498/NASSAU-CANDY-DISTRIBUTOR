# Nassau Candy Profitability & Margin Analysis
# Project Overview

This project analyzes product-level profitability and margin performance for Nassau Candy Distributor. The objective is to identify true profit drivers, detect inefficiencies, and evaluate business risks using a structured, data-driven approach. The analysis follows a complete analytical framework including data cleaning, KPI calculation, profitability metrics, and dashboard-based insights .

# Objectives
Evaluate product-level profitability
Identify high-margin and low-margin products
Analyze division-wise performance
Detect cost inefficiencies and pricing issues
Assess profit concentration and dependency risk
🛠 Tools Used
Power BI (Dashboard & Visualization)
Microsoft Excel (Data Cleaning & Preparation)
#  Key Analysis
🔹 Data Cleaning & Validation
Removed invalid and zero-value records
Handled missing values
Standardized product and division labels
🔹 Profitability Metrics & KPIs
Gross Margin (%)
Profit per Unit
Revenue Contribution
Profit Contribution
Margin Volatility

These metrics helped evaluate efficiency, profitability, and risk across products.

🔹 Product & Division Analysis
Identified top-performing and low-performing products
Compared revenue vs profit across divisions
Detected imbalance between sales and profitability
🔹 Cost vs Margin Diagnostics
Identified high-cost, low-profit products
Highlighted pricing inefficiencies
🔹 Profit Concentration Analysis
Applied Pareto analysis
Found that a small number of products generate most profit
Identified dependency risk on top products
#  Key Insights
Profitability is uneven across products
High sales do not always result in high profit
Business depends heavily on a few products
Certain divisions show lower financial efficiency
